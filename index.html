Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about car shipping and auto transport

How much does it cost to ship a car?

Car shipping costs typically range from $500 to $2,500 depending on several factors: distance, vehicle size, transport type (open vs enclosed), season, and route popularity. Cross-country shipping averages $1,000-$1,500. Get a free instant quote for your specific route and vehicle to see the exact price.

How long does car shipping take?

Transit times vary by distance. Cross-country shipments typically take 7-14 days, while regional moves (under 500 miles) may take 3-7 days. Coastal-to-coastal routes average 8-12 days. We offer expedited shipping options for faster delivery when needed. Weather, traffic, and route specifics can affect exact timing.

Is my vehicle insured during transport?

Yes! All shipments through OK Car Shipping LLC include comprehensive insurance coverage. Our carriers are required to carry liability insurance as mandated by the FMCSA. For additional protection, we offer supplemental cargo insurance options. All vehicles are covered for up to the vehicle's value during transport.

What's the difference between open and enclosed transport?

Open transport is the standard, economical option where vehicles are shipped on open trailers (like you see on highways). It's perfect for daily drivers. Enclosed transport provides complete protection from weather, road debris, and other elements inside a fully enclosed trailer. This is recommended for luxury, classic, exotic, or high-value vehicles.

Do you offer door-to-door service?

Yes! We specialize in convenient door-to-door car shipping. Your vehicle will be picked up and delivered at locations you specify (home, work, etc.). We handle all the logistics for a hassle-free experience. Terminal-to-terminal service is also available if you prefer to drop off/pick up at our facilities.

What items can I leave in my car during shipping?

For safety and compliance with DOT regulations, we recommend removing all personal belongings. Carriers typically prohibit items in the vehicle during transport. Essential items like registration and insurance documents should be kept with you. Any items left in the vehicle are not covered by insurance and are at your own risk.
