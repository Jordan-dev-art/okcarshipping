What is Open Car Transport?

Open car transport is the most common and economical method for shipping vehicles across the country. It involves transporting your vehicle on an open-air trailer that can carry multiple vehicles simultaneously.

Open Transport

  • Most affordable option
  • Carries 6-10 vehicles
  • Safe for daily drivers
  • Fastest availability

Enclosed Transport

  • 30-60% more expensive
  • Carries 2-5 vehicles
  • Maximum protection
  • Limited availability

Open Transport Cost Factors

Understanding what affects the price of open car shipping

Distance

Longer distances typically cost less per mile. Cross-country shipping averages $0.50-$0.75 per mile.

Vehicle Size

Larger vehicles (SUVs, trucks) take up more space and may cost 10-20% more than compact cars.

Season & Timing

Peak seasons (summer, holidays) see higher demand and prices. Book 2-4 weeks in advance for best rates.

Fuel Prices

Fluctuating fuel costs directly impact shipping rates. Prices adjust with current fuel market conditions.

Route Popularity

Popular routes (CA to TX, NY to FL) often have more carriers and competitive pricing.

Vehicle Condition

Running vehicles are easier to load. Non-operational vehicles may require special equipment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about open car transport answered

Is open car transport safe for my vehicle?

Yes, open car transport is very safe for most vehicles. Millions of cars are shipped via open carriers annually with minimal issues. While there's exposure to weather and road debris, professional carriers use secure tie-down systems and experienced drivers. For daily drivers and standard vehicles, open transport provides excellent safety at an affordable price.

How much does open car transport cost?

Open transport costs vary based on distance, vehicle size, season, and route. Average costs range from $400-$700 for short distances (under 500 miles), $700-$1,200 for medium distances (500-1,500 miles), and $1,000-$1,800 for cross-country shipments. Get a free quote for your specific route and vehicle for accurate pricing.

Will my car get dirty during open transport?

Your vehicle may get some road dust, dirt, or bug splatters during open transport, especially on long trips. This is normal and expected. We recommend washing your vehicle before pickup (makes inspection easier) and planning to wash it upon delivery. Most customers find a simple car wash upon delivery addresses any accumulated dirt from the journey.

How long does open transport take?

Transit times for open transport are typically: 2-4 days for distances under 500 miles, 5-7 days for 500-1,500 miles, and 7-14 days for cross-country shipments. Open transport often has faster availability than enclosed transport since there are more open carriers on the road. Weather, traffic, and route specifics can affect exact timing.

Can I ship personal items in my car during open transport?

No, for safety and insurance reasons, personal items should not be left in your vehicle during open transport. Carriers typically prohibit personal belongings in vehicles, and these items are not covered by insurance if lost or damaged. Remove all personal items before pickup, including items in the glove compartment, trunk, and under seats.

When should I choose enclosed over open transport?

Choose enclosed transport for: luxury vehicles ($75,000+ value), classic/antique cars, exotic sports cars, vehicles with custom paint jobs, collectible/rare vehicles, or when shipping during severe weather seasons. For daily drivers, family vehicles, standard cars, trucks, and SUVs, open transport is the practical and economical choice.
