What is Enclosed Car Transport?

Enclosed car transport is the premier method for shipping high-value vehicles in a fully covered, protected trailer. Unlike open transport, your vehicle is completely shielded from weather, road debris, and outside elements throughout its entire journey.

Enclosed Transport FAQ

Answers to common questions about premium vehicle shipping

Is enclosed transport worth the additional cost?

For vehicles valued over $75,000, classics, exotics, or any car with custom paint work, enclosed transport is absolutely worth the investment. The complete protection from weather, road debris, and potential damage provides peace of mind that open transport cannot offer. Additionally, premium insurance coverage and white-glove service ensure your vehicle receives the care it deserves. For many collectors and luxury owners, enclosed transport is the only acceptable method.

What types of enclosed carriers are available?

We offer several types of enclosed carriers: Soft-sided enclosed trailers (fabric sides, lightweight), hard-sided enclosed trailers (aluminum or steel, maximum protection), luxury lift-gate carriers (hydraulic lifts, climate-controlled), and multi-car enclosed transporters (2-5 vehicles). For exotic and extremely low-profile vehicles, we also offer specialized low-boy enclosed trailers with extra clearance.

How far in advance should I book enclosed transport?

Due to limited availability of premium enclosed carriers, we recommend booking 2-3 weeks in advance for standard shipments. During peak collector seasons (April-October), major auction events (Barrett-Jackson, Mecum, RM Sotheby's), or concours shows (Pebble Beach, Amelia Island), booking 4-6 weeks ahead is strongly advised. Our premium concierge team can sometimes accommodate last-minute requests through our VIP carrier network.

What is climate-controlled transport and when do I need it?

Climate-controlled transport maintains consistent temperature and humidity levels inside the enclosed trailer. This is essential for: vintage vehicles with original leather interiors that can crack in extreme temperatures, cars with wooden components (classic wagons, early roadsters), vehicles with museum-quality paint finishes, exotic cars with carbon fiber components, and any vehicle being transported during extreme weather seasons (desert heat, northern winters).

Can I ship a non-running classic car via enclosed transport?

Yes, absolutely. Our enclosed carriers are equipped with hydraulic lift gates and winch systems specifically designed for non-operational vehicles. This is particularly common for restoration projects, museum pieces, and collector cars being transported to specialty shops. Please specify the non-running condition when requesting your quote so we can assign a carrier with the appropriate equipment and provide accurate pricing.

How does insurance work for high-value vehicles?

For vehicles valued over $100,000, we recommend purchasing additional cargo insurance beyond the carrier's standard coverage. Our premium insurance partners offer agreed value policies that cover the full appraised value of your vehicle with zero deductible options. We can provide certificates of insurance prior to shipment for your records. Most collector car insurance policies also extend coverage during professional transport—check with your provider.
