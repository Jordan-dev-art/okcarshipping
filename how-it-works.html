The Complete Car Shipping Process

Understanding how auto transport works can make your vehicle shipping experience stress-free and efficient. This comprehensive guide walks you through each step of the process, from initial quote to final delivery.

1

Get Your Free Quote

Request an accurate, no-obligation car shipping estimate

The first step in the auto transport process is getting a free car shipping quote. This gives you an accurate estimate of what it will cost to ship your car based on several key factors.

What Affects Car Shipping Costs?

  • Distance: Longer distances typically cost less per mile than shorter trips
  • Vehicle Size: Larger vehicles (SUVs, trucks) may cost more than compact cars
  • Transport Type: Open car transport is more affordable than enclosed auto transport
  • Seasonality: Prices may be higher during peak moving seasons (summer, holidays)
  • Route Popularity: Popular routes often have more competitive pricing
  • Vehicle Condition: Running vs. non-running vehicles affect transport method
Pro Tip: Get Multiple Quotes

While we're confident in our competitive pricing, it's always smart to compare quotes from multiple auto transport companies. Look for transparency, no hidden fees, and clear explanations of what's included.

How to Get an Accurate Quote

For the most accurate auto shipping quote, be prepared with:

  • Pickup and delivery ZIP codes
  • Vehicle make, model, and year
  • Vehicle condition (running or non-operational)
  • Preferred transport type (open or enclosed)
  • Desired pickup timeframe
2

Schedule & Book Your Shipment

Secure your spot and choose your preferred dates

Once you've reviewed your car shipping quote and decided to proceed, the next step is to schedule and book your shipment.

Booking Process Details

  • Choose Your Dates: Select your preferred pickup window (typically 1-3 days)
  • Select Transport Type: Decide between open transport (most common) or enclosed transport (for luxury/classic cars)
  • Payment Terms: Most companies require a deposit to secure your booking
  • Paperwork: Complete necessary documentation including the bill of lading
  • Contact Information: Provide accurate phone numbers for pickup and delivery coordination
Important: Booking Lead Time

The car shipping timeline varies by season. For best results and optimal pricing, book your shipment at least 1-2 weeks in advance. During peak seasons (summer, holidays), book 3-4 weeks ahead.

What Happens After Booking?

Once your auto transport is booked:

  • You'll receive a confirmation with all details
  • Your shipment is added to our carrier network
  • We match your vehicle with an appropriate carrier
  • You'll be assigned a dedicated transport coordinator
  • You'll receive carrier information as soon as it's assigned

Booking Lead Time

1-4 weeks depending on season and route

Pickup Window

Typically 1-3 day windows for flexibility

Paperwork

Bill of lading and vehicle inspection report

3

Vehicle Pickup & Inspection

Professional pickup with thorough documentation

This is when the actual vehicle shipping begins. The carrier arrives at your specified pickup location to load your vehicle.

Pickup Day Process

  • Carrier Contact: The driver will call you 24-48 hours before pickup to confirm
  • Vehicle Inspection: A thorough inspection is conducted before loading
  • Documentation: The bill of lading is completed and signed by both parties
  • Loading: Your vehicle is safely loaded onto the carrier using proper equipment
  • Final Confirmation: You'll receive confirmation that your vehicle is en route

Vehicle Inspection Checklist

The carrier will document the condition of your vehicle, including:

  • Existing scratches, dents, or damage
  • Tire condition and tread depth
  • Glass condition (windows, windshield)
  • Interior condition (seats, dashboard)
  • Mileage at time of pickup
  • Special features or modifications
Pro Tip: Document Everything

Take your own photos and videos of your vehicle before pickup. Document any existing damage from multiple angles. This provides additional protection and ensures a smooth claims process if needed.

What to Have Ready at Pickup

  • Vehicle keys (provide all sets if possible)
  • Valid identification
  • Vehicle registration (some carriers require it)
  • Personal items removed from the vehicle
  • Contact information for the driver
4

In-Transit & Tracking

Monitor your shipment in real-time

Once your vehicle is loaded and en route, you can track its progress throughout the auto transport journey.

What to Expect During Transit

  • Transit Times: Vary by distance (see timeline chart below)
  • Communication: Regular updates from your transport coordinator
  • Tracking: Real-time tracking available through our online portal
  • Safety: Your vehicle is secured with professional equipment
  • Insurance: Fully covered throughout the entire journey

Average Transit Times by Distance

Short Distance

Under 500 miles: 2-4 days

Medium Distance

500-1,500 miles: 5-7 days

Cross-Country

1,500+ miles: 7-14 days

Factors Affecting Transit Time

  • Weather Conditions: Severe weather can cause delays
  • Traffic & Road Conditions: Construction and accidents affect timing
  • Carrier Route: Some carriers make multiple stops
  • Pickup/Delivery Locations: Urban vs. rural areas affect scheduling
  • Time of Year: Peak seasons may have longer transit times
Pro Tip: Stay Flexible

While we provide estimated delivery dates, car shipping timelines can vary due to factors beyond our control. Allow a few days of flexibility in your schedule, especially for long-distance moves.

5

Vehicle Delivery & Inspection

Safe delivery and final paperwork

The final step in the car shipping process is the safe delivery of your vehicle to its destination.

Delivery Day Process

  • Advance Notice: The driver will call 24-48 hours before delivery
  • Final Arrival: Driver arrives at the specified delivery location
  • Vehicle Unloading: Safe unloading with proper equipment
  • Final Inspection: Thorough inspection comparing to pickup condition
  • Paperwork Completion: Signing of final documents and bill of lading
  • Payment: Final payment (if balance remains)
  • Keys & Documents: Return of keys and all paperwork

Delivery Inspection Checklist

Before accepting delivery, carefully inspect:

  • Compare condition to pickup inspection report
  • Check for any new damage or issues
  • Verify mileage matches or is reasonable for distance
  • Test that all features and functions work
  • Ensure personal items (if any were left) are still present
  • Confirm the vehicle starts and runs properly
Important: Don't Sign Until Satisfied

Never sign the delivery receipt until you've completed a thorough inspection. Once you sign, you're acknowledging the vehicle was delivered in the condition described. If there are issues, note them on the bill of lading before signing.

After Delivery

  • Keep all paperwork for your records
  • Provide feedback about your experience
  • Consider leaving a review to help future customers
  • Contact us with any post-delivery questions
  • File a claim immediately if you discover damage

Car Shipping Preparation Checklist

Proper preparation ensures a smooth auto transport experience

Vehicle Preparation

  • Wash your vehicle for easier inspection
  • Take photos from all angles before pickup
  • Check tire pressure and inflate as needed
  • Ensure brakes are in working order
  • Test that all lights are functioning
  • Check and top off fluid levels
  • Address any fluid leaks before shipping
  • Remove custom accessories if possible

Personal Belongings

  • Remove ALL personal items from the vehicle
  • Empty glove compartment and center console
  • Remove CDs, DVDs, and media from players
  • Take out toll transponders and parking passes
  • Remove roof racks, bike racks, and carriers
  • Check trunk, under seats, and storage areas
  • Personal items are NOT covered by insurance
  • Leave only factory-installed items

Security & Documentation

  • Provide all sets of keys to the driver
  • Disable or provide instructions for car alarms
  • Leave about 1/4 tank of gas (not full, not empty)
  • Have registration and insurance available
  • Note any special instructions for the driver
  • Document existing damage thoroughly
  • Keep a copy of all paperwork
  • Save contact information for the carrier

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about how car shipping works

How long does the entire car shipping process take?

The complete car shipping timeline typically takes 1-3 weeks from initial quote to delivery. This includes 1-2 days for quote processing, 1-7 days for carrier assignment, and 2-14 days for actual transit time depending on distance. The entire process from booking to delivery usually ranges from 7-21 days.

What documents do I need for car shipping?

You'll need a valid driver's license or ID, vehicle registration (in some cases), and insurance information. The carrier will provide the bill of lading, which is the most important document in the auto transport process. This document serves as a receipt and contract between you and the carrier.

Can I track my vehicle during transport?

Yes, most reputable auto transport companies offer real-time tracking. At OK Car Shipping LLC, we provide tracking through our online customer portal. You'll receive regular updates and can check your vehicle's status 24/7. The carrier will also provide contact information and estimated arrival times.

What happens if my car is damaged during shipping?

All reputable carriers carry cargo insurance. If damage occurs, document it immediately with photos and note it on the bill of lading before signing. Contact your transport coordinator immediately to file a claim. Most claims are resolved within 30 days. This is why thorough pre-shipment documentation is crucial.

Can I ship a non-running vehicle?

Yes, you can ship non-running vehicles, but there are additional considerations. Non-operational vehicles require special loading equipment (winches) and may cost more to transport. You must inform us during the quote process so we can arrange the appropriate equipment and provide accurate pricing.

How much does it cost to ship a car?

Car shipping costs vary based on distance, vehicle size, transport type, and season. Average costs range from $500 for short distances to $2,500 for cross-country enclosed transport. Get a free auto shipping quote for accurate pricing specific to your route and vehicle.
