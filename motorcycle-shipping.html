What is Motorcycle Shipping?

Motorcycle shipping is the professional transport of motorcycles, scooters, ATVs, and other powersports vehicles using specialized carriers and equipment. Whether you're moving across the country, purchasing a bike online, or transporting your motorcycle to a rally or race, professional motorcycle transport ensures your prized possession arrives safely and securely.

Motorcycle Shipping Cost Factors

Understanding what affects your shipping price

Distance

Longer distances typically offer better per-mile rates. Cross-country shipping averages $0.50-$1.00 per mile depending on route.

Motorcycle Size

Larger bikes (baggers, touring, adventure) require more space and may cost 15-25% more than standard cruisers or sport bikes.

Transport Type

Open transport is most economical. Enclosed transport costs 30-60% more but provides maximum protection for valuable bikes.

Season & Timing

Peak seasons (spring-fall) and major rallies (Sturgis, Daytona) see higher demand. Book 2-3 weeks ahead for best rates.

Fuel Prices

Fluctuating fuel costs directly impact shipping rates. Prices adjust with current fuel market conditions.

Crating Requirements

Non-running bikes, parts, or multiple motorcycles may require custom crating, which adds to the total cost.

Motorcycle Shipping Preparation Checklist

Proper preparation ensures safe, damage-free transport

Mechanical Prep

  • Check tire pressure (recommended PSI)
  • Inspect for fluid leaks and repair
  • Ensure battery is secured
  • Check brakes are operational
  • Leave fuel at 1/4 tank or less
  • Disable or note alarm systems
  • Remove aftermarket electronics
  • Secure or remove loose parts

Documentation

  • Take photos from all angles
  • Document existing scratches/damage
  • Record current mileage
  • Have registration available
  • Note any special instructions
  • Keep insurance information ready
  • Save carrier contact details
  • Prepare bill of lading

Final Preparation

  • Remove all personal items
  • Take off saddlebags (if removable)
  • Remove windscreens (if fragile)
  • Fold in mirrors
  • Secure or remove GPS devices
  • Disable anti-theft systems
  • Provide all keys to driver
  • Clean bike for easier inspection

Important Note:

For non-running motorcycles, please inform us during the quote process. Special equipment (winches, lift gates, crating) may be required and will affect pricing and carrier availability.

Motorcycle Shipping FAQ

Answers to common questions about motorcycle transport

How do you secure motorcycles during transport?

Professional motorcycle carriers use specialized equipment including: wheel chocks to hold the front wheel, soft loop straps that attach to the handlebars or frame (never plastic parts), canyon dancers that protect handlebar controls, and additional tie-downs for the rear. All straps are soft-sided to prevent scratching and are carefully tensioned to allow suspension movement without bike shifting.

Can I ship a non-running motorcycle?

Yes, we can ship non-running motorcycles. However, this requires special handling and equipment such as winches, lift gates, or crating. Please inform us during the quote process so we can assign a carrier with the appropriate equipment and provide accurate pricing. Non-running bikes may incur additional fees due to the extra labor and equipment required.

What is motorcycle crating and when is it needed?

Motorcycle crating involves building a custom wooden crate around your motorcycle for maximum protection. This is typically recommended for: extremely high-value motorcycles, non-running bikes, parts or project bikes, multiple motorcycles being shipped together, or when shipping via standard freight (not dedicated auto transport). Crating provides additional security and allows bikes to be shipped on standard freight carriers if needed.

How much fuel should I leave in my motorcycle?

We recommend leaving approximately 1/4 tank of fuel or less in your motorcycle for transport. This reduces weight, minimizes fire risk, and complies with safety regulations. Full tanks can cause fuel to slosh and potentially leak during transit. Empty tanks can lead to condensation. A quarter tank is the perfect balance for safe transport.

Can I leave luggage or accessories on my motorcycle?

We recommend removing all loose items including saddlebags (unless permanently attached), tail bags, tank bags, GPS units, phone mounts, and any aftermarket accessories. These items can shift during transport and cause damage. If saddlebags are permanently attached, ensure they are empty and securely fastened. Personal items left on the bike are not covered by insurance.

How far in advance should I book motorcycle shipping?

For best availability and rates, we recommend booking 1-2 weeks in advance for standard shipments. During peak riding season (April-October), major motorcycle rallies (Sturgis, Daytona, Laconia), or holiday periods, booking 3-4 weeks ahead is strongly advised. For enclosed transport or specialty services, allow additional lead time to secure the right carrier.
